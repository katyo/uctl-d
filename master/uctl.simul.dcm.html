uctl simul

DC motor model

Simple model of brush direct current motor.

Model input:

  • U_r - supply voltage, V
  • T_l - load torque, N⋅m

Model output:

  • \omega_r - rotor speed, rad/S

Model parameters:

  • R_r - rotor resistance, Ohm
  • L_r - rotor inductance, H
  • \Psi_s - stator magnetic flux, Wb
  • J_r - rotor inertia, Kg⋅m2

Model state:

  • i_r - rotor current, A
  • T_e - electric torque, N⋅m
  • \omega_r - rotor speed, rad/S
  • E_b - back EMF, V

Basic model equations:

U_r - E_b = R_r i_r + L_r \frac{d i_r}{d t} (1)

T_e - T_l = J_r \frac{d \omega_r}{d t} (2)

T_e = \Psi_s i_r (3)

E_b = \Psi_s \omega_r (4)

where:

  • U_r - rotor voltage, V
  • E_b - back EMF, V
  • R_r - full active resistance of rotor including brushes, Ohm
  • L_r - rotor coil inductance, H
  • i_r - rotor current, A
  • T_e - electrical torque of rotor, N⋅m
  • T_l - load torque at shaft, N⋅m
  • J_r - inertia of rotor, Kg⋅m2
  • \Psi_s - nominal magnet flux of stator, Wb
  • t - time, S

Motor identification:

\Psi_s = \frac{U_r - R_r i_r}{\omega_r}
